# NSU 2.0 Research Collection

## Overview
This document contains comprehensive research on the German Nationalsozialistischer Untergrund (NSU) 2.0, collected from various reliable sources including Wikipedia, Der Spiegel, Deutsche Welle, Bundeszentrale für politische Bildung (bpb), Deutscher Bundestag, and Mediendienst Integration.

## What is NSU 2.0?

NSU 2.0 refers to a series of threatening letters and emails sent since August 2018, signed with "NSU 2.0" - a reference to the original Nationalsozialistischer Untergrund (NSU), a right-wing terrorist group active in Germany from 2000-2007.

### Key Facts:
- **Timeline**: Started August 2018, ongoing
- **Signature**: "NSU 2.0" and variations like "NationalSozialistischeOffensive" (NSO), "Elysium", "Staatsstreichorchester", "Wehrmacht"
- **Targets**: Approximately 70 different individuals and 60 institutions across Germany and Austria
- **Primary targets**: People publicly engaged against racism, antisemitism, for refugees and migrants - mostly women, often with migration backgrounds

## The Original NSU (Context)

The original NSU was a right-wing terrorist group consisting of:
- **Uwe Böhnhardt** (died 2011)
- **Uwe Mundlos** (died 2011) 
- **Beate Zschäpe** (convicted 2018)

### NSU Crimes (2000-2007):
- 10 murders (8 Turkish-German, 1 Greek-German small business owners, 1 police officer)
- 15 robberies
- 3 bomb attacks
- 2 attempted murders

## Academic Analysis

According to bpb analysis, NSU represents:
- **Continuity**: Connection to long history of right-wing extremist violence
- **Discontinuity**: New type of violence combining elements of "hot" and "cold" terrorism
- **Characteristics**:
  - Systematically planned attacks
  - Ideological self-purpose (not primarily communicative)
  - Focus on physical elimination of ideological enemies
  - Long-term stable group structure (unusual for right-wing groups)

## Recent Legal Developments (2023-2025)

### February 2024: Police Investigation Discontinued
- **Frankfurt Prosecutor's Office**: Discontinued investigations against two police officers in NSU 2.0 complex
- **Date**: December 7, 2023 (announced February 2024)
- **Reason**: "Insufficient suspicion" could not be established against the officers
- **Background**: One officer was logged into computer when unauthorized data query occurred; colleague was also on duty
- **Appeal**: Complaint filed against the decision
- **Key Issue**: Personal data accessed from police computer in 1st Frankfurt police precinct was used in August 2018 threat fax to Seda Başay-Yıldız

### March 2024: Federal Court of Justice Decision
- **Bundesgerichtshof**: Final ruling on Alexander M. case (March 21, 2024)
- **Outcome**: Frankfurt Regional Court verdict (November 17, 2022) upheld with minor modifications
- **Final Sentence**: 5 years and 10 months imprisonment for Alexander M.
- **Key Findings**:
  - Alexander M. acted alone as sole perpetrator
  - 67 cases of threatening emails, faxes, and SMS between August 2018 and March 2021
  - Used "NSU 2.0" signature and "Heil Hitler" slogans
  - Manipulated police and officials by pretending to be prosecutor/relative
  - Stored police station phone numbers with notes like "Never call as prosecutor again!"
  - No connections to police officers or supporters found
  - Four unauthorized police data queries occurred during crime period, but no link to perpetrator

### Ongoing Concerns Despite Legal Conclusions
- **Data Source Mystery**: How Alexander M. obtained personal data remains unclear
- **Police Data Queries**: Four confirmed unauthorized queries from police computers during threat period
- **Right-wing Chat Groups**: Discovery of right-wing extremist chat group within Frankfurt police precinct
- **Systemic Issues**: Questions about broader right-wing networks in security agencies remain

## Current Context (2025)

- **Historical Context**: Highest level since Nazi rule according to Left Party inquiry
- **Violent Crimes**: 1,443 violent crimes recorded in 2024
- **Underreporting**: Authorities systematically underestimate right-wing motives
- **State Apparatus Infiltration**: Evidence of far-right structures within government agencies

### Political Climate Impact
- **Normalization**: Right-wing extremist views increasingly socially acceptable
- **Government Response**: Interior Minister Nancy Faeser expanded police powers while attacking refugees
- **Political Attacks**: Crimes against elected officials increased from 4,047 to 4,923 in 2024

### Historical Recognition Issues
- **Delayed Recognition**: Many right-wing murders only classified as such after decades
- **Examples**: 
  - Horst Pulter murder (1995) took 20 years for right-wing classification
  - Alexander Selchow murder (1991) still not properly classified
  - Walter Lübcke murder (2019) by right-wing extremist
- **Statistics Gap**: Amadeu Antonio Foundation lists 219 right-wing murders since 1990, but only 116 in official statistics

### NSU 2.0 in Current Context
